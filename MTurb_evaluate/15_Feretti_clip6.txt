<div class="panel-body">
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>When we communicate, our speech is a combination of&nbsp;words and expressive vocal gestures. &nbsp;Most of our conscious attention is on the words, but what about the <strong><em>expression</em></strong>? &nbsp;Expression in our voices&nbsp;tells us as much as the words. &nbsp;For example, expression&nbsp;can tell us what the speaker thinks about what they are saying, whether they are asking a question, whether the speaker is serious or sarcastic,&nbsp;and the speaker&#39;s emotional state. &nbsp;What does our vocal expression say?</p>

<p>You can help us answer this important question&nbsp; For this task you will:</p>

<ul>
	<li><strong style="font-family: Verdana, Geneva, sans-serif; font-size: 14px;">Answer some demographic questions.</strong></li>
	<li><strong style="font-family: Verdana, Geneva, sans-serif; font-size: 14px;">Listen to a recording of a person talking.</strong></li>
	<li><strong><strong style="font-family: Verdana, Geneva, sans-serif; font-size: 14px;">Provide 3&nbsp;or more keywords describing the expression you hear in the voice... &nbsp;That&#39;s it!</strong></strong></li>
</ul>

<p>Sound interesting? Read on...</p>

<p><b style="font-family: Verdana, Geneva, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; background-color: rgb(251, 251, 251);">This section of the survey will ask you to listen to a short voice recording, and provide keywords describing what you hear. &nbsp;Pay attention to the EXPRESSION in the voice. &nbsp;NOT the words. &nbsp;We are interested in HOW someone is speaking.</b><span style="font-family: Verdana, Geneva, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; background-color: rgb(251, 251, 251);">&nbsp; </span></p>

<p>Some examples of keywords that describe HOW someone is speaking are emotional words like: happy, sad, angry, etc. &nbsp;Again, pay attention to HOW the person speaks, not what they say.</p>

<p>Other examples of keywords describing expression in the voice are words that describe what is happening to the speed of the voice, loudness in the voice, or whether the sound is high or low. &nbsp;Words in this category might include &quot;fast&quot;, &quot;loud&quot;, or&nbsp;&quot;speeding up&quot;. &nbsp;</p>

<p>Finally, expression can involve the quality of the voice. &nbsp;Words like &quot;whispering,&quot; &quot;growling,&quot; &quot;breathy,&quot; &nbsp;or &quot;creaky&quot; are words that describe voice quality.</p>
